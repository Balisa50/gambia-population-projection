# Policy Brief — The Gambia's Population to 2074

**What an independent, census-based projection tells us — and why it differs from
the UN's numbers.**

*Abdoulie Balisa · Statistics, KNUST · based on open data; methods and code fully
reproducible.*

---

## The headline

Using The Gambia's **2024 Census** and the same modelling family the United
Nations uses, this study projects the country's population to **about 4.7 million
by 2074** (with a plausible range of **4.35–4.98 million**), up from 2.42 million
in 2024.

## Three things policymakers should know

**1. The UN's current figures are about 13% too high for The Gambia.**
The UN's *World Population Prospects 2024* put the 2023 population near 2.73
million, but the 2024 Census counted 2.42 million. Because the UN's projection was
finalised *before* the census, plans calibrated to UN numbers are working with a
population roughly **300,000 people too large today** — and about **0.7 million
too large by 2074** (5.35M vs our 4.66M). National plans should be re-based on the
census.

**2. The Gambia is entering its "demographic dividend" window.**
The dependency ratio — the number of children and elderly per 100 working-age
adults — is projected to fall from **77 today to about 49 by the 2070s**. This is
the demographic moment that powered the growth of East Asia: a large, growing
working-age population relative to dependents. **It is an opportunity, not a
guarantee** — it pays off only if those workers find productive jobs and education.

**3. Ageing is coming, even in a young country.**
While the share of children falls, the number of older people grows fast: the
**old-age dependency ratio roughly triples** (from about 5 to about 18 per 100
working-age adults) by 2074. The Gambia will need pension and elderly-health
capacity that barely exists today — and the time to build it is during the
dividend window, not after.

## How confident are we?

Every figure comes with an explicit **range**, not a single guess. The projection
engine was *validated* by reproducing the UN's own projection to within 1%. Life
expectancy is projected to rise from about 66 today to roughly **75 years by
2074**. The main uncertainties are migration (volatile for The Gambia) and the
exact detailed age structure of the new census, which will refine these numbers.

## What this is, and isn't

This is independent, transparent research built entirely on **public data**, with
all code and data openly available for scrutiny and reproduction. It is **not** an
official government or UN projection. Its value is as a timely, census-based
cross-check that anyone can verify — and a demonstration that rigorous demographic
analysis of The Gambia can be done locally.
