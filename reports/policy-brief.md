# Policy Brief: The Gambia's Population to 2074

**An independent, census-based projection, and why it differs from the UN's
numbers.**

*Abdoulie Balisa. Statistics, KNUST. Built on open data; all methods and code are
reproducible.*

---

## The headline

Using the 2024 census and the same modelling the United Nations uses, this study
projects The Gambia's population at about 4.7 million by 2074, somewhere between
4.35 and 4.98 million. That is up from 2.42 million in 2024.

## Three things worth knowing

**1. The UN's current figures are about 13% too high for The Gambia.**
The UN put the 2023 population near 2.73 million. The 2024 census counted 2.42
million. The UN's projection was finished before the census came out, so any plan
built on UN numbers is working with roughly 300,000 too many people today, and
about 0.7 million too many by 2074 (5.35 million in the UN's version against 4.66
million here). National plans should be re-based on the census.

**2. The Gambia is entering its demographic dividend.**
The dependency ratio is the number of children and elderly for every 100
working-age adults. It is set to fall from about 77 today to about 49 by the
2070s. This is the same window that powered the rise of East Asia, when a large
and growing working-age population can lift a country quickly. It is an
opportunity, not a guarantee. It only pays off if those workers find real jobs and
real schooling.

**3. Ageing is coming, even to a young country.**
As the share of children falls, the number of older people grows fast. The
old-age dependency ratio roughly triples, from about 5 to about 18 per 100
working-age adults, by 2074. The Gambia will need pension and elderly-health
capacity it barely has today, and the cheapest time to build it is during the
dividend years, not after.

## How confident is this?

Every figure comes with a range, not a single guess. The projection engine was
tested by reproducing the UN's own projection, and it matched to within 1%. Life
expectancy is projected to rise from about 66 today to roughly 75 by 2074. The
biggest remaining unknowns are migration, which is volatile for The Gambia, and
the detailed age breakdown of the new census, which will sharpen these numbers
once it is published in full.

## What this is, and what it is not

This is independent, transparent research built entirely on public data, with all
code and data open for anyone to check. It is not an official government or UN
projection. Its value is as a timely, census-based second opinion that anyone can
verify, and as a sign that serious demographic work on The Gambia can be done
locally.

---

*Full methods and results are in the accompanying research report. Data: UN World
Population Prospects 2024 and the Gambia Bureau of Statistics 2024 census.
Methods: Lee-Carter mortality modelling, Bayesian inference, and a
cohort-component projection.*
